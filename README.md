## Decoding Extract Ratios: What They Actually Mean

The "15x" or "50x" language on extract labels confuses most buyers.

Here's the actual definition. A 15x extract used 15 grams of raw leaf to produce 1 gram of extract. The number represents the raw material reduction ratio — not a potency multiplier.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Extraction ratio is NOT a reliable potency indicator on its own. A 15x extract from high-alkaloid leaf may be more potent than a 25x extract from weak leaf. Always verify mitragynine percentage from a third-party COA — never buy on ratio numbers alone.

### Ratio vs. Real Potency: What Matters

### Step 1: Confirm AKA Certification

Visit [americankratom.org](https://americankratom.org) and verify current certification status.

Extract brands especially should be AKA certified. Their manufacturing complexity requires higher quality control infrastructure.

### Step 2: Verify Mitragynine Percentage

Request the COA. Find the mitragynine percentage column.

Standard powder: 1.0–1.7%. Legitimate extract: 5–45%+ depending on grade.

If the COA shows standard powder-range percentages on an "extract" product — it's not extract.

### Step 3: Confirm Residual Solvent Testing

Find the solvent panel on the COA. Ethanol and water extractions should show zero detectable residuals.

Any chemical solvent results above safe trace levels is an immediate disqualifier.

### Step 4: Check Independent Lab Identity

Google the testing lab named on the COA.

Is it a real, independent facility? Do they have a public website? Have other kratom brands used them?

An unknown "lab" with no web presence suggests in-house testing disguised as third-party verification.
